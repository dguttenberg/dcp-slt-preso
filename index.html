The extensions
Forces extend
Brand Gravity.

Brand Gravity is the center. Forces are separately-purchasable products that work on their own and work better connected.

A Force is not a department and not a framework. It is a real product someone buys — scoped, priced, differentiated. Growth happens by adding Forces as the agency identifies new capabilities worth productizing.

Qualification for a Force
Costs more. Works standalone. Is made better when connected to Brand Gravity. If a new capability fails any of the three, it is not a Force — it is a service or an instrument.

The commercial shape
Two ways clients meet us.
Brand Gravity is how
we distinguish between them.
We qualify every client against one question: does this brand need to operate coherently at scale, or does it need specific work done? The answer shapes which version of DCP the client gets.
Version 01
Brand-immersion clients
Brand Gravity is the operating model
We insist on it. The Core gets stood up at the start of the engagement and feeds every workstream. Priced into the scope as either a line item or as part of overhead, depending on how the client's finance team wants to see it.
Forces priced separately
Velocity and future Forces are their own commercial shapes — hours, output, subscription, or combination. Discounted or enhanced when connected to the client's Brand Gravity.
Version 02
Transactional clients
Services without the center
Campaigns, projects, tightly-scoped programs. The client buys agency services — creative, strategy, data, production — priced the traditional way. No Brand Gravity.
Instrument benefit flows through
Our teams use the instruments they use internally. Clients feel the speed and quality without being hooked into the Core. If the relationship grows, the door to Brand Gravity opens.
Operating principleYear 1 pricing reflects the value of the intelligence infrastructure Brand Gravity encodes. Efficiency arguments are deferred to Year 2 with proof. We do not sell savings we have not yet delivered.

APPENDIX · A2
How it shows up in the agency
Volume expands.
Craft stays with the craftspeople.
Brand Gravity changes every department differently. Each discipline defines what their own work becomes as context arrives pre-loaded and instruments absorb the connective work. Scope capacity grows without burning the people who make the brand.
Account
From brand memory-keeper to orchestrator and trust-holder. Context lives in Gravity, not in someone's head. The relationship and the judgment sit with the account lead.
Strategy
From briefing helper to opportunity-surfacer. Encodes the brand, periodically reviews it, and mines growth missions the client hasn't asked for yet.
Creative
From context reconstruction to sharper thinking with ownable tools. Richer context arrives before the work starts, and the time saved goes into the thinking that matters.
Data & Intelligence
From episodic reports to curated intelligence feeds. Findings get ingested into Brand Gravity on a cadence. The team's craft compounds, doesn't expire.
Production
From executing briefs to better iteration and execution. More shots on goal, faster, with brand coherence built in rather than checked for afterward.
Media
From audience reconstruction to brand-aligned targeting. Encoded audience truth feeds every plan. Brand strategy and media strategy stay in sync.
New Business
From pitching frameworks to demoing the system. The first conversation qualifies brand-immersion versus transactional and shapes everything that follows.
Technology
From service provider to peer discipline. Owns the platform, builds the raw instruments, maintains the library that the rest of the agency plugs into.
This is about efficiency. It is about brand cohesion at scale much more.
